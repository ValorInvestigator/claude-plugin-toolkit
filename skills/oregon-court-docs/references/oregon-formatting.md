# Oregon Circuit Court Formatting Reference

## Page Break and Continuation Rules

### Signature Block Placement
**Never have the signature block on a page by itself.** This is a critical rule - an isolated signature page could potentially be detached and attached to another document.

### // Continuation Marks
When space remains at bottom of page and content continues on next page:
- Use // marks to fill remaining lines
- Left-aligned (not centered)
- Minimum of 2 lines of // marks
- Indicates to reader that more text follows

### What Must Stay Together
These elements should never be split across pages:
- Date line and signature block
- Section header and first paragraph of that section
- Final numbered item in a list and the signature block
- "IT IS FURTHER ORDERED" paragraph and judge signature (on proposed orders)

### Placement Strategy
Place // marks BEFORE the content that needs to stay together:
- After body text, before final order paragraph and judge signature
- After Prayer item 4, keeping item 5 + date + signature together

## Typography

### Font Requirements
- **Primary font**: Courier New (monospace, universally accepted)
- **Body text**: 12pt (24 half-points in docx-js)
- **Footer**: 10pt (20 half-points)
- Never use decorative fonts or varying sizes within body

### Page Layout
- **Paper**: US Letter 8.5" x 11"
- **Margins**: 1 inch all sides
- **DXA conversions**: 1 inch = 1440 DXA

## Case Caption Format

The case caption uses a two-column visual structure:
- Left side: Party names and designations
- Right side: Case number, document title, special notations
- NO closing underline at bottom of caption

### Caption Tab Stops
```
Position 4680 DXA (3.25"): Parentheses column
Position 5040 DXA (3.5"): Case information column
```

### Party Designations
- "Plaintiff," or "Plaintiffs," (with comma)
- "Defendant." or "Defendants." (with period, last party)
- Indent designations ~10 spaces from left margin

### Document Titles in Caption
Common titles that appear in the caption right column:
- SUMMONS
- COMPLAINT
- MOTION FOR [specific relief]
- DECLARATION OF [name]
- MEMORANDUM IN SUPPORT
- ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE
- PROPOSED ORDER

### Special Notations
Add below document title when applicable:
- (ORS [statute number])
- ORAL ARGUMENT REQUESTED
- EXPEDITED HEARING
- JURY TRIAL DEMANDED

## Document Body

### Opening Paragraphs

**Motions**:
"COMES NOW Plaintiff, [Name], Pro Se, and moves this Court for [relief sought]."

**Declarations**:
"I, [Name], declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the State of Oregon as follows:"

**Complaints**:
"Plaintiff [Name] alleges as follows:"

### Section Headers
- Centered, bold, underlined
- Examples: POINTS AND AUTHORITIES, FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS, PARTIES, JURISDICTION

### Numbered Points
Format: "[Number]. [Title]: [Content]"
- Number in bold
- Title in bold and underlined
- Content in regular weight
- First line indented 0.5 inch

### Sub-points
Format: "[letter]. [Content]"
- Lowercase letters (a, b, c)
- Left indent 1 inch (1440 DXA)
- No first-line indent

### WHEREFORE Paragraph
- "WHEREFORE" in bold
- Followed by specific relief requested
- List relief items with appropriate legal citations

## Signature Block

### Format
```
DATED: ____________________


____________________________________
[NAME], Pro Se
[Street Address]
[City], OR [ZIP]
```

### Spacing
- 2-3 blank lines between date and signature line
- No space between signature line and name
- Single space between address lines

## Page Footer

### Standard Format
```
[DOCUMENT TITLE] - Page [current] of [total]
```

### Examples
- SUMMONS - Page 1 of 1
- PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE - Page 2 of 3
- DECLARATION OF LEVI BAKKE - Page 1 of 4

## Oregon Public Records Law Citations

### Key Statutes
- **ORS 192.311-192.478**: Oregon Public Records Law
- **ORS 192.431**: Judicial review of denials
- **ORS 192.431(2)**: Expedited hearing requirement
- **ORS 192.431(3)**: Attorney fees provision
- **ORS 192.411(2)**: Seven-day challenge period

### Standard Language

**Expedited Hearing**:
"Pursuant to ORS 192.431(2), this case is to take precedence over other causes and shall be assigned for hearing and trial at the earliest practicable date."

**Attorney Fees**:
"Pursuant to ORS 192.431(3), Plaintiff is entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs."

## Common Defendants

### State Agencies
- Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS)
- Oregon Department of Justice (DOJ)
- Oregon Health Authority (OHA)

### Service Addresses
Always include both agency and DOJ for state agency defendants:
1. Agency address with director name
2. DOJ address with Attorney General name

## Exhibits

### Reference Format
In text: "(Ex. [number])" or "(Exhibit [number])"

### Declaration References
"As shown in Exhibit 5, attached hereto..."
"See Declaration of [Name], ¶ [number]"
