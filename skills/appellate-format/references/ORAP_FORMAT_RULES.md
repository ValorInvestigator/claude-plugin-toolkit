## 1. GENERAL FORMAT RULES (ORAP 5.05)

### 1.1 Paper & Page Size
- **Paper size**: 8.5 x 11 inches (letter size), uniform throughout
- **Paper type**: White bond paper, minimum 20-pound weight
- **Opacity**: If double-sided printing, paper must have sufficient opacity (no bleed-through)
- **Covers**: 8.5 x 11 inches

### 1.2 Font Requirements
- **Type**: Proportionally spaced type ONLY (typewriter-style monospaced type is outdated but if used, max 10 characters per inch)
- **Permitted fonts**:
  - **Times New Roman** (14 point minimum)
  - **Arial** (14 point minimum)
  - **Century Schoolbook** (14 point minimum)
- **Minimum size**: 14 point for BOTH body text AND footnotes
- **Prohibited**:
  - Reducing or condensing typeface to increase word count
  - Briefs printed entirely or substantially in UPPERCASE
- **Color**: Black print only (hyperlinks excepted)

### 1.3 Margins
- **Inside margin** (binding side): 1.25 inches
- **Outside margin**: 1.0 inch
- **Top margin**: 0.75 inches
- **Bottom margin**: 0.75 inches
- **Maximum printable page area**: 6.25 inches wide x 9.5 inches tall

### 1.4 Spacing
- **Body text**: Double-spaced
- **Quotations**: Double-space above AND below each block quotation
- **Footnotes**: May be single-spaced (but still 14-point minimum font)

### 1.5 Page Numbering
- **Location**: Top of page, within 3/8 inch from the top edge
- **Style**: Consecutive Arabic numerals throughout the brief
- **Excerpt of Record**: Uses "ER-1, ER-2, ER-3" format (separate pagination)
- **Appendix**: Uses "App-1, App-2, App-3" format (separate pagination)

### 1.6 Word Count Limits

#### Court of Appeals:
| Brief Type | Word Limit | Alternative Page Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Opening Brief | 10,000 words | 35 pages |
| Answering Brief | 10,000 words | 35 pages |
| Combined Answering/Cross-Opening | 16,700 words (answering portion max 10,000) | — |
| Combined Cross-Answering/Reply | 10,000 words (reply portion max 3,300) | — |
| Reply Brief | 3,300 words | 10 pages |
| Supplemental Brief | 5 pages max | 5 pages |
| Appendix | 25 pages max | 25 pages |

#### Supreme Court:
| Brief Type | Word Limit | Alternative Page Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Opening Brief | 14,000 words | 50 pages |
| Answering Brief | 14,000 words | 50 pages |
| Combined Answering/Cross-Opening | 22,000 words (answering portion max 14,000) | — |
| Combined Cross-Answering/Reply | 12,000 words (reply portion max 4,000) | — |
| Reply Brief | 4,000 words | 15 pages |
| Supplemental Brief | 5 pages max | 5 pages |
| Appendix | 25 pages max | 25 pages |

**NOTE on page limits**: Page limits apply ONLY when a party is unable to determine if the brief complies with the word-count limitation. Word count is the primary measure.

#### What is EXCLUDED from word count:
- Headings
- Front cover
- Table of contents (index)
- Table of authorities (authorities index)
- Excerpt of record
- Appendix
- Certificates (word count certificate, certificate of filing, certificate of service)
- Signatures

### 1.7 Cover Page Requirements

#### Required Information on Front Cover:
1. Case title (full title from trial court, including ALL parties)
2. Party designations at trial level (plaintiff, defendant, etc.)
3. Party designations on appeal (appellant, respondent, cross-appellant, etc.)
4. Case number — BOTH courts (trial court case number AND appellate case number)
5. Identification of which party is filing the brief
6. Originating court name and county
7. Name of trial judge who signed the judgment
8. Litigant contact information per ORAP 1.30:
   - For attorneys: name, bar number, firm name, address, phone, email
   - For self-represented parties: name, mailing address, telephone number
9. Filing month and year

#### Cover Colors (for paper filing):
- **Note**: Cover color requirements historically applied to paper-filed briefs. With mandatory eFiling for OSB members, cover colors apply primarily to the first page/cover page of the PDF. The traditional color scheme:
  - Opening Brief (Appellant): Blue cover
  - Answering Brief (Respondent): — (check current rule)
  - Reply Brief: — (check current rule)
  - Petition for Review Response: Orange cover
  - Amicus Brief: — (check current rule)

> **VERIFY**: The 2025 ORAP may have modified or eliminated physical cover color requirements given mandatory eFiling. Check ORAP 5.05(4)(a) and ORAP 16.15 for current requirements.

### 1.8 Binding & Physical Filing
- **Preferred binding**: Binder clips
- **Acceptable binding**: Staples
- **Copies**: File ONE original with the Appellate Court Administrator
- **Service**: Serve ONE copy on ALL parties
- **Proof of service**: Must appear on last page or back cover

### 1.9 Final Page Requirements
The final page of the brief must include:
- Author's name and signature (person who wrote the brief)
- Law firm name(s) representing the party (if applicable)
- Identification of parties represented

## 2. OPENING BRIEF REQUIREMENTS (ORAP 5.40 + 5.45)

### 2.1 Required Sections (In Order)

The appellant's opening brief must contain the following sections under **separate headings**, in this order:

#### A. COVER PAGE
- See Section 1.7 above for all required cover elements

#### B. TABLE OF CONTENTS (Index)
- Lists all sections of the brief with page numbers
- Lists all headings and subheadings

#### C. TABLE OF AUTHORITIES (Index of Authorities)
- All cases cited, with page references
- All statutes cited, with page references
- All other authorities (constitutional provisions, rules, treatises, law review articles)
- Organized by category (Cases, Statutes, Other Authorities)

#### D. STATEMENT OF THE CASE (ORAP 5.40)
The statement of the case must include, in this order under separate subheadings:

1. **Nature of the Action/Proceeding**: Statement WITHOUT ARGUMENT of:
   - Type of action or proceeding
   - Relief sought
   - In criminal cases: indictment or information with applicable statute citations

2. **Nature of the Judgment**: Statement WITHOUT ARGUMENT of:
   - Nature of the judgment sought to be reviewed
   - Whether trial was before the court or a jury

3. **Jurisdictional Basis**:
   - Statutory basis of appellate jurisdiction
   - Supporting authority (especially where jurisdiction may be novel or doubtful)

4. **Timeliness of Appeal**:
   - Date of entry of judgment in trial court register
   - Date notice of appeal was served and filed
   - If appeal was filed more than 30 days after judgment entry: explanation of why the appeal is timely
   - Other facts relevant to appellate jurisdiction

5. **Questions Presented on Appeal**:
   - Brief statement WITHOUT ARGUMENT of the issues on appeal

6. **Summary of Argument**:
   - Concise summary of the arguments in the body of the brief

7. **Statement of Facts**:
   - Concise summary WITHOUT ARGUMENT of ALL facts material to the appeal
   - Must include references to the transcript or record

#### Additional Statement of Case Elements (when applicable):
- **Administrative Review Cases**: Nature of agency action and jurisdictional basis
- **Discretionary Review Requests**: Statement if seeking Court of Appeals discretion to retry or make new factual findings
- **Dissolution Cases**: Marriage date, parties' ages, support terms
- **Significant Motions**: Description of motions filed and disposition
- **ORAP 10.30 Statement**: Optional explanation of whether issues meet listed factors

#### E. ASSIGNMENTS OF ERROR (ORAP 5.45)

**CRITICAL SECTION** — This is where appeals are won or lost.

Each assignment of error must be:
1. **Separately stated** with numbered headings
2. **Precisely identified** — what specific ruling, decision, or action is being challenged
3. **Categorized** — whether it is legal, procedural, factual, or other
4. **Arranged** per Appendix 5.45 illustrations

For EACH assignment of error, you must include:

**Preservation of Error**: Demonstrate the issue was properly raised below:
- When the issue was raised
- How it was presented (motion, objection, etc.)
- The lower court's response/ruling
- Record citations with pertinent quotations
- Page references to transcript or excerpt of record

**Standard of Review**: Identify the applicable standard:
- De novo
- Substantial evidence
- Abuse of discretion
- Statutory alternative
- Must include legal citations supporting the standard

**For evidentiary rulings**: Quote or summarize the evidence and identify where it was admitted or excluded.

#### F. ARGUMENT
- Arguments should be organized by assignment of error
- Arguments may be combined when multiple assignments present the same legal question
- Plain error arguments must explain why review is warranted even absent preservation
- Cite to the record (transcript, exhibit, document) for all factual assertions

#### G. CONCLUSION
- State the specific relief requested

#### H. CERTIFICATES
- Certificate of Compliance with Word Count (ORAP 5.05(2)(b))
- Certificate of Filing
- Certificate/Proof of Service

#### I. SIGNATURE
- Author's name and signature
- Law firm name(s)
- Parties represented

#### J. EXCERPT OF RECORD (bound with brief or separately — see Section 5)

#### K. APPENDIX (if any — see Section 6)

## 3. ANSWERING BRIEF REQUIREMENTS

The respondent's answering brief follows the same general format as the opening brief with these modifications:
- Must respond to each assignment of error
- May include additional assignments of error (cross-appeal)
- Same word count limits apply (10,000 COA / 14,000 SC)
- Same formatting requirements apply

---

## 4. REPLY BRIEF REQUIREMENTS

- Limited to matters raised in the answering brief
- Word count: 3,300 (COA) / 4,000 (SC)
- Page limit alternative: 10 pages (COA) / 15 pages (SC)
- Same formatting requirements apply
- Reply brief is OPTIONAL — not required

## 5. EXCERPT OF RECORD (ORAP 5.50)

### 5.1 Who Must File
- **Required**: All appellants EXCEPT self-represented parties
- **Self-represented parties**: May omit the excerpt entirely, OR may include only the judgment/order and relevant rulings (nothing more)
- Parties are encouraged to confer and file a joint excerpt

### 5.2 Required Contents
The excerpt MUST contain:
1. The judgment or order being appealed
2. Written or oral rulings addressing assignments of error
3. Relevant pleadings, transcript excerpts, exhibits, and filings necessary for understanding the case
4. Materials showing preservation of error (if preservation is disputed)
5. **eCourt Case Information register of actions** (if case arose in Oregon circuit court) — appears LAST in excerpt
6. In criminal conditional plea cases: documentation of reserved issues

### 5.3 Format Requirements
- **Paper**: 8.5 x 11 inch white paper
- **Reproduction**: Clear, legible black image by any copying/duplicating process
- **Pagination**: Consecutive, starting with **ER-1** (ER-1, ER-2, ER-3, etc.)
- **Organization**: Chronological order (except case register appears last)
- **Index**: Must begin with a chronological index describing:
  - Each item included
  - Where the item can be found in the trial court record
  - The excerpt page number where the item appears
- **Bookmarks**: Must include bookmarks in electronic filings
- **Content**: Must be COPIES of actual documents — NOT summarized or paraphrased
- Must comply with ORAP 5.05 specifications

---

## 6. APPENDIX (ORAP 5.52)

- **Maximum**: 25 pages
- **Pagination**: Uses "App-1, App-2, App-3" format
- **Content**: Constitutional provisions, statutes, rules, and other authorities central to the appeal that may not be readily available to the court
- Must comply with ORAP 5.05 specifications
- **Excluded from word count**

## 7. NOTICE OF APPEAL (ORAP 2.05)

### 7.1 Form
- Must substantially follow Appendix 2.05 form
- Only the ORIGINAL needs to be filed (no copies required)

### 7.2 Required Contents
The notice of appeal must contain:

1. **Case Title**: Complete trial court title with ALL parties named, their trial court designations (plaintiff, defendant, etc.), appeal designations (appellant, respondent), and BOTH case numbers (trial court + appellate)

2. **Heading**: "NOTICE OF APPEAL" or "NOTICE OF CROSS-APPEAL"

3. **Identification of Judgment**:
   - What judgment or portion of judgment is being appealed
   - Which court and county
   - Name of trial judge(s) who signed it

4. **Adverse Parties**: Designation of adverse parties on appeal

5. **Contact Information**: Per ORAP 1.30:
   - Self-represented: name, mailing address, telephone number
   - Attorney: name, bar number, firm, address, phone, email

6. **Record Designation**: Specify:
   - Trial court testimony to be transcribed
   - Instructions
   - Exhibits
   - Audio/video recordings
   - Include hearing dates if transcription requested

7. **Statement of Points**: Plain and concise statement of intended arguments on appeal
   - NOT required if ALL testimony and instructions are designated for inclusion

8. **Timeliness**: If filing exceeds 30 days post-judgment, explain why appeal is timely

9. **Jurisdictional Citation**: If appellate jurisdiction is questionable, cite authority

10. **Proof of Service**: Requirements vary by case type (civil, criminal, juvenile)

11. **Certificate of Filing**: Specifying filing date with the Administrator

12. **Attached Judgment**: Copy of the judgment, decree, or order being appealed, plus any pertinent jurisdictional orders

### 7.3 Filing Deadline
- **General**: 30 days after entry of judgment in the trial court register
- See ORS 19.255 for specific deadlines by case type
- **Cross-appeal**: 10 days after service of the notice of appeal

### 7.4 Where to File
- File with the **Appellate Court Administrator** (State Court Administrator's Office)
- Address: Oregon Judicial Department, Appellate Court Records Section
- Phone: 503-986-5555

### 7.5 Filing Fee
- Check current fee schedule at courts.oregon.gov
- Fee waiver/deferral applications available for indigent parties

## 8. MANDAMUS PETITION (ORAP 11.05)

### 8.1 Overview
ORAP Chapter 11 governs original proceedings in the Oregon Supreme Court, including petitions for writs of mandamus.

### 8.2 Format Requirements
- Must follow ORAP 5.05(3) formatting standards (same font, margins, spacing as briefs)
- Must substantially match the form in Appendix 11.05
- Filed as a unified single PDF if eFiled

### 8.3 Required Content

#### Title Page / Caption:
- Case caption with writ type specified (alternative or peremptory)
- Identifying numbers from lower court proceedings
- **For challenges to judge actions**: Use lower court title format, designating applicant as "relator" and opponent as "adverse party"
- **For other mandamus actions**: "State ex rel [Relator], Plaintiff-Relator, v. [Defendant]"

#### Supporting Statement must include:
1. Concise factual summary of the underlying dispute
2. Explanation of timeliness
3. Justification of why circuit court relief was not or could not be sought
4. Demonstration that appeal is NOT an adequate remedy (this is critical — mandamus is extraordinary relief)

#### Documentation:
- Proof of service on ALL adverse parties
- Proof of service on ALL other lower court participants
- If state entity involved: proof of service on the **Attorney General**

### 8.4 Accompanying Materials

1. **Memorandum of Law**: Supporting arguments and citations following ORAP 7.10 standards
2. **Excerpt of Record**: If a record exists, must include excerpt per ORAP 5.50(5)
3. **Copy of challenged order/decision**: Must attach the order or written decision being challenged

### 8.5 Special Requirements

- **Stay Requests**: If seeking a stay, caption must include "STAY REQUESTED" and must show either:
  - That a stay was sought from the lower court, OR
  - Why requesting one from the lower court would be futile
- **Confidential Materials**: Attachments containing sealed or confidential information must comply with ORAP 8.52

### 8.6 Service
- Serve all adverse parties
- Serve all parties to the underlying judicial or administrative proceeding
- Service sufficient if compliant with ORCP 9 B
- If challenging state action: serve the Attorney General

## 9. ELECTRONIC FILING (ORAP Chapter 16)

### 9.1 Mandatory eFiling
- **Mandatory** for all active Oregon State Bar members
- **Optional** for self-represented (pro se) parties — may file electronically OR on paper
- eFiling system: Oregon Appellate Courts eFiling System (via courts.oregon.gov)

### 9.2 Document Format Requirements
- **Format**: Portable Document Format (PDF) only
- **Text searchability**: Must allow text searching, copying, and pasting
  - **Exception**: Text-searchability does NOT apply to pro se supplemental briefs filed under ORAP 5.92
- **File size**: Maximum 25 megabytes per file
  - If document exceeds 25 MB: split into two or more documents before filing
- **Unified PDF**: Must be submitted as a single, unified PDF (not principal document + attachments), with limited exceptions

### 9.3 What Goes Into the Single PDF
Examples of unified PDF contents:
- Notice of appeal + judgment being appealed + certificate of service
- Petition for judicial review + agency order + certificate of service
- Motion + affidavit/declaration (if any) + certificate of service
- **Mandamus petition** + copy of challenged order/decision + certificate of service
- Brief + excerpt of record + appendix + certificates

### 9.4 eFiling Logistics
- **Filing deadline**: System accepts filings through **11:59 PM** on due dates
- **Filing date**: Document is considered filed when received by the system
- **Confirmation**: Three emails — "Submitted," "Received," and "Accepted" or "Rejected"
- **Technical issues**: Court system problems may allow filing date to relate back (ORAP 16.25(4)); YOUR equipment problems do NOT excuse late filing
- **No filing fee for eFiling itself** — but statutory filing fees for appeals/motions still apply

### 9.5 Self-Represented eFiling
- Self-represented parties can register for public accounts
- Request case access within 2 business days of submission
- All the same PDF format requirements apply

### 9.6 Help Desk
- **Hours**: 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday-Friday (excluding court closure days)
- **Phone**: 503-986-5582

## 10. SELF-REPRESENTED LITIGANT RULES

### 10.1 General Accommodations
Oregon appellate courts provide several resources and accommodations for self-represented (pro se) parties:

- **Not required to eFile** — may file on paper
- **Excerpt of record relaxation** (ORAP 5.50): Self-represented parties may:
  - OMIT the excerpt of record entirely, OR
  - Include ONLY the judgment/order and relevant rulings (nothing more)
- **Text-searchability exemption**: Pro se supplemental briefs under ORAP 5.92 do not need to be text-searchable
- **Fee waiver/deferral**: Applications available for indigent parties
- **Oral argument**: Temporary rule allows self-represented parties to argue their own cases in the Court of Appeals

### 10.2 Available Resources
- Current Oregon Rules of Appellate Procedure (full text online)
- Standards of Review document
- Sample Briefs & Motions (with downloadable examples)
- Appellate Courts Style Manual (citation and formatting guidance)
- State of Oregon Law Library (legal research)
- Brief Bank (digital repository of sample briefs): https://cdm17027.contentdm.oclc.org/digital
- "What to Expect at Oral Argument" guide
- "Oral Argument — Quick Reference Guide"
- Filing fee waiver/deferral application forms

### 10.3 Pro Se Supplemental Brief (ORAP 5.92)
This rule applies when a party has court-appointed counsel but disagrees with counsel's brief:
- Client or counsel may move for leave to file a supplemental pro se brief
- Must be filed within **28 days** of the motion
- Must follow proper appellate form "as nearly as practicable"
- Must identify specific issues as assignments of error
- **Limited to 5 pages** (unless court orders otherwise)
- Excerpt of record may only include materials per ORAP 5.50(2) not already in opening brief
- Counsel may assist ONLY with: correcting typographical errors, preparing copies, serving parties, filing
- "Pro se" = "for oneself" or "on one's own behalf" — brief represents client's own work product

### 10.4 Practical Notes for Self-Represented Parties
- The court applies the SAME substantive legal standards to pro se filings
- Format rules still apply (font, margins, spacing, word count) — but the court may show some leniency on technical formatting
- **CRITICAL**: Assignments of error must still be properly identified and preserved — this is where most pro se appeals fail
- **CRITICAL**: Jurisdictional requirements (timeliness, proper notice) are strictly enforced regardless of pro se status
- Consider using the sample briefs as templates
- The Appellate Court Records Section (503-986-5555) can answer procedural questions (but cannot give legal advice)

## 11. WORD COUNT CERTIFICATE

Every brief must include a certificate of compliance with ORAP 5.05(2)(b), certifying the brief complies with the applicable word-count limitation. The certificate should state:

- The word-processing program used
- The word count as determined by that program
- Which items were excluded from the count (headings, cover, index, authorities, excerpt, appendix, certificates, signatures)

Example format:
```
CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE WITH BRIEF LENGTH LIMITATIONS

I certify that this brief complies with the word-count limitation in
ORAP 5.05(2)(b). The word count of this brief as computed by
[Microsoft Word / other program] is [X,XXX] words.

____________________________
[Name]
```

  - [ ] Summary of Argument

- [ ] Margins: 1.25" inside, 1.0" outside, 0.75" top/bottom

### 12C. Mandamus Petition Checklist

- [ ] Caption per Appendix 11.05
- [ ] Writ type specified (alternative or peremptory)

- [ ] Proper party designations (relator, adverse party, defendant)
- [ ] Concise factual summary
- [ ] Timeliness explanation
- [ ] Why circuit court relief was not/could not be sought
- [ ] Why appeal is not an adequate remedy
- [ ] Memorandum of law with citations (ORAP 7.10)

- [ ] Copy of challenged order/decision
- [ ] Proof of service on ALL adverse parties
- [ ] Proof of service on ALL lower court participants
- [ ] Proof of service on Attorney General (if state entity)
- [ ] If requesting stay: "STAY REQUESTED" in caption
- [ ] If requesting stay: evidence of lower court stay request or futility explanation
