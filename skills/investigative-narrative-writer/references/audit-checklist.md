# Final Self-Audit Checklist

Run this checklist before returning ANY Publishable Prose output. Every time. No exceptions.

---

## Step 1: Delete Any Sentence That...

- [ ] Explains what the chapter is doing
- [ ] Tells the reader how to feel
- [ ] Repeats a fact the scene already conveys
- [ ] Sounds like outline language
- [ ] Sounds like workshop commentary
- [ ] Sounds like a closing argument instead of reported narrative
- [ ] Addresses the reader directly ("Think about that," "Listen to him")
- [ ] Summarizes the significance of a scene that already landed
- [ ] Uses a rhetorical abstraction loop ("That is what..." / "This is where..." / "That is why...")
- [ ] References a chapter number, section name, or the structure of the work itself
- [ ] Is an empty emphasis sentence that only signals importance ("That matters." / "That is the danger.")
- [ ] Repeats a thematic label the reader already knows from the section title or earlier prose
- [ ] Uses first person for emotional steering or moral commentary instead of reporting activity
- [ ] Ends a section with an abstract verdict instead of a concrete image, quote, or fact

---

## Step 2: Full Read-Through Checks

- [ ] **Scene first, mechanism second** -- every section opens with a human moment before institutional violence
- [ ] **Human anchor before institutional violence** -- the reader cares about the person before seeing what was done
- [ ] **Factual integrity** -- no silently altered facts (dates, names, weights, dollar amounts)
- [ ] **Controlled escalation** -- no later-chapter evidence spent early
- [ ] **No meta prose** -- no sentences about the writing itself
- [ ] **No direct reader address** -- no second-person commands
- [ ] **No rhetorical abstraction loops** -- no abstract verdict sentences
- [ ] **No generalized institutional voice in body sections** -- save for conclusions
- [ ] **Concrete-to-abstract ratio** -- no more than two abstract paragraphs without a concrete anchor
- [ ] **No structural self-reference** -- no chapter numbers, section names, or "this article" in prose
- [ ] **No empty emphasis sentences** -- cut "That matters" / "That is the danger" and strengthen the line before it
- [ ] **Thematic labels used sparingly** -- not repeated after section title
- [ ] **First person limited to reporting activity only** -- no emotional steering or moral commentary
- [ ] **Verdict quarantine** -- every section/chapter ends on concrete image, quote, or fact (not abstract judgment)
- [ ] **Landing lines** -- images, quotes, or irreversible turns, not summaries

---

## Step 2b: Craft-Level Checks (Sentence and Sensory)

- [ ] **Mute senses present** -- at least one scene per section uses sound, smell, or touch (not just visual)
- [ ] **Status details** -- characters shown through specific possessions, clothing, brands, habits (not generic adjectives like "elderly" or "kind")
- [ ] **To-be diet** -- search for "is," "was," "were," "are," "been" + "-ing" verbs and replace with active constructions
- [ ] **Strong final words** -- the most important word in each sentence lands at the end, not buried in the middle
- [ ] **Quote diet** -- all direct quotes are 6-20 words; bloated/institutional language is paraphrased with only the revealing words quoted
- [ ] **Proportional comparisons** -- large numbers, statistics, and dosages are compared to something concrete and relatable
- [ ] **Pizza slice pacing** -- sections open narrow (human detail) and widen (institutional scope), never the reverse
- [ ] **Adverb sweep** -- search for "-ly" words and replace with stronger singular verbs
- [ ] **No show-off sentences** -- cut any sentence that exists to sound pretty rather than advance the narrative
- [ ] **No boring parts** -- if your own eyes glaze over a paragraph, cut it and replace with action or dialogue

---

## Step 2c: The 10% Solution (Revision Only)

When revising a draft, apply this mechanical pass:

- [ ] Count the word count of the input draft
- [ ] Target: cut at least 10% of that word count
- [ ] Hit list searched in order: passive voice, adverbs, bloated quotes, show-off sentences, boring parts
- [ ] Final word count recorded and compared to input
- [ ] If less than 10% was cut, make another pass -- you are not trying hard enough

---

## Step 3: Final Quality Gate

Ask these three questions:

1. **Does this read like a lawyer's closing argument?** If yes, rewrite. It should read like a journalist walking the reader through a house of horrors one room at a time.

2. **Does the reader feel like they are discovering the facts?** If the prose feels like a briefing, restructure so evidence unfolds as narrative discovery.

3. **Would this survive legal review?** Every factual claim must be grounded in exact quotes or documented evidence. Every hedge must be in place. Every unverified detail must be flagged or removed.

If anything fails this audit, fix it before returning the draft.
